Your Recovery Timeline

What to expect, day by day.

Every recovery is different. What's described below represents common experiences — your own timeline may vary. Use this as a general frame of reference, not a strict schedule.

Day of Surgery
Rest. Nothing more.
  • Rest as much as possible — your body is working hard
  • Apply your Cold Therapy Jaw Wrap as directed by your surgeon
  • Keep your head elevated — even when resting
  • Sip water and clear fluids — stay hydrated
  • Use gauze as directed and change as needed
  • Avoid any physical activity
Day 1 — 24 Hours After
Swelling peaks. That's expected.
  • Swelling often peaks on day 1–2 — this is normal
  • Continue cold therapy in cycles as your surgeon advised
  • Begin electrolyte packets to stay replenished
  • Sip calming herbal tea — warm, not hot
  • Apply arnica recovery gel gently to outer jaw area
  • Keep lips moisturized with your medicated lip balm
Days 2 – 3
Discomfort begins to ease.
  • Swelling should begin to slowly reduce
  • Begin gentle oral rinsing with your salt water healing rinse
  • Use the oral rinse syringe carefully to keep the area clean
  • Continue soft foods and fluids — no chewing
  • If your care team has cleared Arnica pellets, use as directed on the packaging
  • Rest as needed — fatigue during this phase is common
Days 4 – 7
Turning the corner.
  • Most patients notice meaningful improvement this week
  • Continue rinsing consistently after meals
  • Continue any supplements your care team has cleared — see the supplements section below
  • Soft foods can gradually broaden — follow your surgeon's guidance
  • Light activity may resume if your surgeon approves
  • Attend your follow-up appointment as scheduled
Your Kit

What's inside & how to use it.

Here's a simple reference for when and how to use each item in your kit.

🧊
Cold Therapy Jaw Wrap
Apply to jaw and cheeks in 20-minute cycles during the first 48 hours. Do not apply directly to skin — use a thin cloth layer.
🌿
Arnica Recovery Gel
Apply gently to the outer jaw and cheek area. Avoid open wounds or incisions. A comfort support for the outer tissue.
🩹
Wound Care Gauze
Use as directed by your surgeon to manage the surgical site. Fold and place gently — avoid excessive pressure.
💉
Oral Rinse Syringes
Begin gentle irrigation as directed by your surgeon — typically starting day 3–5. Use with warm salt water or healing rinse.
🧴
Salt Water Healing Rinse
A gentle, alcohol-free rinse. Swish softly — no forceful rinsing — to keep the mouth clean without disturbing the healing site.
🫧
Recovery Washcloths
Use warm or cool as needed for facial comfort. Gentle cleansing around the mouth area when brushing is limited.
💋
Medicated Lip Balm
Mouth breathing during recovery causes dry, cracked lips. Apply frequently. A small comfort that makes a real difference.
⚡
Electrolyte Packets
Mix with water and sip regularly. Staying hydrated and replenishing electrolytes supports your overall recovery.
🍵
Calming Herbal Tea
Warm (not hot) herbal tea soothes and calms. Chamomile and similar blends are gentle on the digestive system during recovery.
🧦
Recovery Comfort Socks
Warmth and comfort during rest days. A small thing that signals to your body: it's time to relax and heal.

Recovery Tips

Small things that help more than you'd expect.

  • 1
    Keep your head elevated. Even while sleeping, propping your head up reduces swelling and pressure. Use an extra pillow or a wedge.
  • 2
    Hydrate consistently. Sip water regularly, even when you don't feel thirsty. Your electrolyte packets help replenish what your body needs.
  • 3
    Eat soft, cool foods. Think yogurt, smoothies, applesauce, broth, and soft scrambled eggs. Avoid anything hot, crunchy, or that requires chewing.
  • 4
    Rinse gently — not forcefully. When you begin rinsing, swish with minimal pressure. Forceful rinsing can dislodge the healing tissue.
  • 5
    Let yourself rest. Fatigue after surgery is completely normal. Your body is working hard beneath the surface. Sleep and downtime are productive.
  • 6
    Keep lips moisturized. Dry lips are one of the most overlooked discomforts during recovery. Your medicated lip balm is there for a reason — use it often.
Know the Difference

Normal recovery vs. when to call your surgeon.

Some discomfort is expected. Knowing what's typical — and what's a signal to call your provider — gives you confidence during recovery.

✓ Generally Normal Swelling that peaks around day 1–2 then gradually reduces

Mild to moderate soreness managed with medication

Some bruising on the jaw or cheeks

Slight oozing for the first 24 hours

Fatigue and low energy for the first few days

Difficulty opening your mouth wide initially
⚠ Contact Your Surgeon If You Notice Heavy or uncontrolled bleeding that does not slow

Fever above 101°F (38.3°C)

Severe or worsening pain after day 3

Pus, unusual discharge, or strong odor from the site

Numbness or tingling that develops after surgery

Difficulty swallowing or breathing
Important

If you experience anything that concerns you — trust your instincts and call your provider. This guide is a general comfort reference only. Your surgeon knows your specific procedure and is your primary point of contact for clinical questions.
